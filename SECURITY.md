# BB369TECH Security Policy

BB369TECH values the security, continuity, and responsible use of its public websites, platforms, forms, and digital services.

Suspected security issues should not be published in a public GitHub Issue, Discussion, pull request, social media post, or other public channel. Reports should be submitted privately through the official BB369TECH contact page:

https://bb369tech.com/contact.html

A report should identify the affected public page or service and provide enough information for the issue to be understood without including unrelated personal information, confidential business information, credentials, payment information, or other sensitive material.

Unauthorized access, destructive testing, service disruption, credential testing, data collection, impersonation, malicious uploads, automated abuse, or attempts to access non-public systems are not permitted. Anyone who encounters private information, credentials, or confidential records should stop immediately and avoid copying, retaining, altering, or disclosing the information.

BB369TECH may review reports, restrict affected features, update public services, contact relevant providers, or take other reasonable action where appropriate. Submission of a report does not create a contract, employment relationship, authorization to continue testing, guaranteed response time, public recognition, or entitlement to compensation.

BB369TECH does not currently operate a public bug bounty program. Any reward, recognition, or other arrangement requires separate written confirmation.

Reports made in good faith should focus on protecting users, businesses, services, and information. Threats, extortion, public pressure, unauthorized disclosure, or claims of affiliation will not be treated as responsible security reporting.

This policy may be updated as BB369TECH services and public systems change. The latest version published through the official BB369TECH repository should be treated as the current policy.
